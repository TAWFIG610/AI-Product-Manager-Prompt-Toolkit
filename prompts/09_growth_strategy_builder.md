# 09. Growth Strategy Builder

## Strategic Context
Growth is not a marketing campaign; it is a product loop. A 50-year PM veteran knows that paid acquisition is a drug that gets more expensive over time. The only sustainable growth is **organic product loops**—where a user using the product naturally generates new users or data, compounding growth without increasing acquisition spend.

---

## The Master Prompt

```markdown
Act as a Principal Growth Architect and Venture Strategist with 20+ years of experience building viral engines and distribution systems. Build a comprehensive growth strategy for the following product:

Product Description: [PRODUCT DESCRIPTION]
Target Growth Goal: [GROWTH GOAL, e.g., Acquire 50,000 active users in 6 months]
Budget Constraints: [BUDGET CATEGORY, e.g., Bootstrapped/Scrappy vs. Funded]

Deliver 3 distinct, structurally diverse growth strategy options (do not provide variations of the same marketing idea; focus on product-led growth, content/SEO loops, and viral loops):

For each growth strategy, provide:

1. STRATEGIC WEDGE & VALUE PROPOSITION:
   - Core concept in a single sentence.
   - The specific "wedge" customer segment and why they are susceptible to this approach.
   - The psychological trigger that motivates them to act.

2. PRODUCT-LED GROWTH (PLG) MECHANISM:
   - What features, hooks, or flows must we add to the product to support this growth?
   - How does a user's normal activity in the product invite others? (e.g., collaboration, shared artifacts, referral bonuses).

3. COMPUNDING GROWTH LOOP FORMULATION:
   - Define the loop step-by-step:
     1. New User Action ->
     2. Product Output/Artifact ->
     3. Distribution/Visibility ->
     4. Prospect Trigger -> (loops back to 1).
   - What is the compounding engine (e.g., viral loop, SEO/content loop, data loop)?

4. MONETIZATION COUPLING:
   - How does this growth strategy naturally funnel users toward paid tiers or upgrade decisions?

5. RISKS & ASSUMPTIONS:
   - What is the single biggest assumption that could cause this strategy to fail?
   - How do we validate or falsify this assumption within 14 days?

6. 90-DAY EXECUTION PLAYBOOK:
   - Month 1: Foundation (what infrastructure to build).
   - Month 2: Alpha Launch (testing with 50 pilot users).
   - Month 3: Scale & Optimization (tweaking variables).
   - Leading indicator to watch at Day 30 to know if the loop is working.
```

## Follow-Up / Deep-Dive Prompts

### Follow-Up 1: Deep Viral Loop Design
```markdown
For the proposed Viral Growth strategy, detail the exact sharing mechanics:
1. Define the sharing trigger: At what exact moment in the user flow does the user feel the highest value, prompting them to share?
2. Write the copywriting templates for the referral email/message.
3. Define the K-Factor equation parameters for this loop: K = (number of invites sent per user) × (conversion rate of invites). How do we push K above 1.0?
```

### Follow-Up 2: AARRR Funnel Optimization
```markdown
Analyze the proposed Growth Strategy across the Pirate Metrics (AARRR) framework:
- Acquisition: How do they discover us?
- Activation: What is the onboarding milestone?
- Retention: Why do they return?
- Referral: How do they invite others?
- Revenue: How do we extract value?
Identify the single step where we will lose the most users and write 3 product solutions to fix this drop-off.
```

## 50-Year PM Wisdom
* **Viral Loops Need Value, Not Just Incentives**: Giving someone "$10 to invite a friend" is a transaction, not a loop. It burns cash and attracts low-quality users. A real loop is organic: when I share a Google Doc, I am sharing it because I need to collaborate, not because Google paid me. The product use-case itself *forces* the referral.
* **Wedge Deep, Not Wide**: Don't try to build a growth strategy for everyone. Build a strategy that dominates a single, tightly-knit, passionate community. Once you own them, they will carry you to the next market segment.
