# 13. Go-to-Market Plan

## Strategic Context
A Go-to-Market (GTM) plan is not a launch day marketing blast. A 50-year PM veteran knows that a launch is a single point in time, whereas GTM is an ongoing process of aligning product, marketing, sales, and support to acquire and retain customers. The core of a premium GTM plan is **positioning**—how you frame your value in a way that makes competitors look outdated or overly complex.

---

## The Master Prompt

```markdown
Act as a Chief Marketing Officer (CMO) and Head of Product Marketing (PMM) with 20+ years of experience launching software products. Create a comprehensive, execution-ready Go-to-Market (GTM) plan for the following launch:

Product/Feature Name: [NAME]
Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) & Target: [ICP DESCRIPTION]
Core Value Proposition: [ONE-SENTENCE VALUE PROP]
Budget Category: [ZERO-BUDGET / SCRAPPY / ENTERPRISE]
Launch Timeframe: [LAUNCH TIMEFRAME]

Deliver a GTM plan structured as follows:

1. LAUNCH GOALS & OBJECTIVES:
   - Define 3 specific, measurable KPIs for 30, 60, and 90 days post-launch (e.g., adoption rate, customer acquisition cost, activation rate).

2. VALUE PROP & MESSAGING FRAMEWORK:
   - Headline: One high-impact line that states the unique value.
   - Sub-headline: Clarifies what the product is, for whom, and the primary benefit.
   - Key Value Pillars (Max 3): For each pillar, write:
     - Feature Name -> Direct Customer Benefit -> Value Metric.
   - Objection Handling: Top 3 objections customers will raise, and the exact messaging to counter them.

3. CHANNEL STRATEGY & MARKETING FLYWHEEL:
   - Identify the top 3 marketing/distribution channels to prioritize (e.g., SEO, product-led sharing, developer evangelism, cold outbound).
   - Detail the launch assets required for each channel.

4. PRE-LAUNCH READINESS CHECKLIST:
   - Deliver a matrix covering readiness actions across departments: Product/QA, Marketing/Copy, Support/CS Training, Legal/Privacy, and Sales Enablement.

5. CHRONOLOGICAL PLAYBOOK (T-7 days to T+30 days):
   - T-7 Days: Internal dry run, staging checks, press embargoes.
   - T-24 Hours: Production release (behind feature flag), team standups.
   - Launch Day: Hourly playbook (announcements, emails, social blasts, monitor telemetry).
   - T+7 Days: Feedbacks logs, review tickets, optimization sprint.
   - T+30 Days: Goal review, retention cohort audit.

6. KILL & PIVOT CRITERIA:
   - Define the quantitative indicators (e.g., high crash rates, negative user feedback, zero conversion) that would trigger a rollback or pivot.
```

## Follow-Up / Deep-Dive Prompts

### Follow-Up 1: Copywriting Campaign Pack
```markdown
Based on the messaging framework generated above, write the actual marketing copy:
1. A 3-step Launch Email Sequence (Teaser, Launch Day, 3-Days Post-Launch).
2. A 5-tweet Twitter/X Launch Thread focused on explaining the problem and showing the solution (GIF/Visual placeholders included).
3. A Product Hunt Launch Description and Maker's Comment.
```

### Follow-Up 2: Sales & Support Enablement Guide
```markdown
Write a 1-page Internal Enablement Guide for our Customer Support and Sales teams:
1. FAQs: 5 common questions customers will ask about pricing, integrations, and migration.
2. Demo Script: A 3-step script for sales reps to showcase this feature on a live call in under 3 minutes.
3. Feature Limitations: What the feature CANNOT do, so teams don't over-promise.
```

## 50-Year PM Wisdom
* **Don't Blow Your Budget on Day 1**: The biggest launch mistake is spending all your energy and budget on Launch Day. Launch Day is a spike; the real work is the steady-state acquisition that follows. Save half your marketing and engineering resources for the post-launch optimization sprint, where you fix the friction discovered by your first 1,000 users.
* **Train Your Front Line**: Your Support and Sales teams are the face of your product. If they don't understand the new feature, how it works, and how to sell it, your launch will fail, regardless of how good the marketing video is. Enable them before you hit "Publish."
